HTML tags

  1. html - The html tag marks the beginning of an HTML document.
  2. head - The head tag contains information about the document, such as its title, author, and keywords.
  3. title - The title tag specifies the title of the document.
  4. meta - The meta tag specifies additional information about the document, such as its description, language, and character encoding.
  5. body - The body tag contains the main content of the document.
  6. h1 - The h1 tag marks a heading at the highest level.
  7. h2 - The h2 tag marks a heading at the second highest level.
  8. h3 - The h3 tag marks a heading at the third highest level.
  9. h4 - The h4 tag marks a heading at the fourth highest level.
  10. h5 - The h5 tag marks a heading at the fifth highest level.
  11. h6 - The h6 tag marks a heading at the sixth highest level.
  12. p - The p tag marks a paragraph.
  13. br - The br tag creates a line break.
  14. em - The em tag marks emphasized text.
  15. strong - The strong tag marks strong text.
  16. b - The b tag marks bold text.
  17. i - The i tag marks italic text.
  18. u - The u tag marks underlined text.
  19. a - The a tag marks a hyperlink.
  20. img - The img tag marks an image.
  21. table - The table tag marks a table.
  22. tr - The tr tag marks a row in a table.
  23. td - The td tag marks a cell in a table.
  24. ul - The ul tag marks an unordered list.
  25. li - The li tag marks an item in an unordered list.
  26. ol - The ol tag marks an ordered list.
  27. li - The li tag marks an item in an ordered list.
  28. div - The div tag marks a generic container element.
  29. span - The span tag marks a generic inline element.
